Music to some people is an appreciation for the ear, but for Sayyi it is an escape to greatness. Born in the middle of five children on the south side of Chicago, Sayyi Hakim McDonald is the only one with a non-collegiate artistry. Some would say he was meant to be an entertainer. Always the center of attention, he was a performing arts student that excelled at being in front of the camera. As he reached his teenage years young Sayyi discovered a hustler mentality not limiting him to other ways to make money while working on his music part-time. Afraid that he would become the typical product of his environment his parents decided to move him to Nigeria at 15.

In Nigeria he had the leeway to be innovative falling in love with the art of music and his own euphonious abilities. Through considerable time to himself, he was able to develop an expertise for storytelling and started chronicling his rhymes. His platform is to promote positive energies and open others’ minds to be who they were meant to be. Musically he demonstrates his views and experiences by candor. Fresh craftsmanship is always enticing to real music lovers. Sayyi will do just that, with his gift to capture an audience through cunning wordplay. Familiar in the industry, he has written and worked with successful artists such as Diddy, DJ Mustard, Tinashe, E40, Jeremih, Miley Cyrus, and more. His plan is not to be overenthusiastic but humble and well-rounded, taking over the Midwest then the nation. His music will move people in a different direction to teaching how to live for the next and not the now. Following his release as a recording artist Sayyi, wishes to become a philanthropic advocate for Africa specifically the youth.

“My heart is doing its own thing. I hope to be remembered as the drum major for passion and Freedom.”
